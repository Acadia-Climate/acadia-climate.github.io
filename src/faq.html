Frequently Asked Questions

Can't find an answer to your question here? Please contact us and we'd be happy to help!

What is your eligibility criteria?

We work with developers issuing removal credits in registries endorsed by the Integrity Council for the Voluntary Carbon Market (ICVCM) Core Carbon Principles (CCPs). For now, we are only able to consider developers that have a project that has issued its first credits.

How does your financing process compare to other options?

Time is money (and opportunity costs). Leveraging our automated application and underwriting, our process is orders of magnitude faster compared to any other financing option available to developers today like equity, prepayments, or streaming financing.

How do your financing costs compare to other options?

Our proprietary automated underwriting model translates into low overhead costs for us, and we pass the savings to you.

What do I need to apply for a loan?

Simply answer a few questions about your company and project and upload your project documents.

Can I apply if my business credit doesn't have a formal credit history?

Yes, we encourage startups and non-profits to apply!

How long do I have to repay a loan?

We offer flexible terms, but generally expect repayment in up to one year. After one year we would enter a cure period where we may help you sell your credits.

What advance rate can I expect?

Between 20% and 70% of the value of your credits, priced at prevalent benchmarks.

What can I do with financing from Acadia?

  • Cover pre-issuance costs like registry fees, validation and verification bodies (VVBs) costs, and others.
  • Finance payments to key project stakeholders like communities, farmers, or other landowners.
  • Warehouse your credits for months in anticipation of rising prices.
  • General working capital uses, such as:
    • Buyer Seasonality: For example, your carbon credits are issued in Q2 but your customer will only buy carbon credits after completing their annual GHG inventory in Q4.
    • Developer Seasonality: Developers of Agriculture, forestry and other land uses may want to start or expand a project during the planting season. Waiting for credits to sell may cause the developer to miss that window.
    • Input Purchases: Financing the purchase of agricultural, forestry, or mechanical inputs such as seeds, fertilizer, equipment, among others.
